Arts and Crafts by Nicole - Recommendations

 

Recommendations

After using materials from a variety of different manufacturers with both hits and misses, the following are the ones I recommend if you're looking for a specific art or craft item:

 

Paint

I prefer thick paint for its texture, pigmentation and opacity and so I love Liquitex's Heavy Body Acrylic line. Liquitex also makes thinner paints and oil paints. One down side is the price, but Liquitex also makes pains under their Basic line which is cheaper. However if you are willing to pay more money, you'll definitely get amazing quality paint from Liquitex.

 

 

Paintbrushes

Simply Simmons has such a large range of paintbrushes, quite possibly in any shape you desire. You can buy their brushes separately so you can choose the sepcific ones you want. I own many brushes from Simply Simmons and they are quite durable, lasting me over 3 years for continuous painting.


 

Canvases

Artist's Loft provides a variety of sizes and canvas types to suit your needs. Whether you are painting with acrylic, oil or watercolour paint, Artist's Loft is more than adequate in making sure your paintings look like works of art.

 

Sketch Pencils

My favourite sketch pencils come from Staedtler. The pencils are consistent and allow for fine drawings and smudging. Staedtler also makes great watercolour pencils and fineliners. Overall, I am always very satisfied with all their products.

 

Oven Baked Clay

Sculpey is a well known company for oven baked clay. I love how there are so many shades and tints for every colour, and it bakes evenly without resulting in any discolouration from your creation. What you make is what you get!


 

Air Dry Clay

It is difficult to find air dry clay that comes in different colours. Majority of air dry clays are white and require painting if you want colour. However, Makin's provides an array of colours so you won't need to paint them. Unfortunately Makin's is not sold locally so you will have to place an order online.

 

 

As well, I recommend the following locations to purchase your materials:

 

Michaels
www.michaels.com

Michaels quite possibly has any art and craft material you can think of, in great quality too. Their prices may be a little more expensive but there are weekly coupons to help save money.

 

Fabricland
www.fabricland.ca

Fabricland has hundreds of different prints on different materials. They sell fabric by the length, providng you the conveneicnce to buy only what you need.

 

Dollarama
www.dollarama.com

If you're looking for inexpensive materials that don't need to be the best quality, Dollarama is great for those with a budget, especially for children's crafts.
